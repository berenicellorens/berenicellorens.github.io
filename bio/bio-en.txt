Biography

Berenice Llorens is a multidisciplinary artist, composer and DJ from Córdoba, Argentina.

Through her work, she develops an artistic research on musical composition and the transformations of the creative process the transformations of the creative process according to the temporal, visual and auditory perception of the the environment we inhabit. Based on deep listening and nature, he develops techniques of sound experimentation and improvisation for sound experimentation and free improvisation to compose in a free, intuitive and non-genre-labeled way. She develops and dialogues with diverse artistic expressions such as drawing, poetry and video drawing, poetry and video art.

In her solo project, he dabbles in experimental electronic music and mixes analog and digital techniques and instruments, processes, voices and field recordings. His work includes formats such as performances, Dj sets, electronic music, sound works and improvisations that oscillate between minimal, techno, break minimal, techno, break, experimental and ambient.

Within the academic context he studied Piano, Musical Composition (UNC) and Graphic Design. She also takes, seminars and masterclasses in the field of Audio Programming and Postproduction.

In 2020 she released Kriptonîa Split vol. XVI with the Argentinean label Kriptonîa. She participated in festivals such as Earth Days Arts Model, Now Net Arts and her music has resonated in Berlin, England and Switzerland.

Berenice is part of collaborative artistic projects such as So Bery Nice, an electronic music duo; Marmotas Dreams, an experimental improvisation duo; "toros", a duo that explores sound territories that coexist between the digital, the corporeal and the spatial; and the women artists collective Vesica Piscis. She is also active in her role as guitarist in electroacoustic music ensembles such as the Sonic Arts Ensemble (Ohio State University)

In 2021, she received support from the Fondo Nacional de las Artes program for her project Marmotas Dreams. During the same year, she has been selected by the Instituto Nacional de la Música Argentina INAMU through the open call for sound experimenters. This distinction has allowed him to participate in the Artlab Artistic Residencies Program by Amplify D.A.I. in collaboration with the British Council, working together with the Brazilian digital artist Carol Santana. The final work will be presented in different festivals and programs of digital creativity around the world.
